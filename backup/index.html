Why Japan?

If you have ended up here somehow, chances are that you don't need much convincing that you should travel Japan (like a local...expat!). However, if you haven't made up your mind, consider:

Japan is interesting no matter where you go

As a country, Japan has really paid attention to preserving and promoting their historic sights as well as their natural sceneries.

The developed tourism industry makes it very easy to travel to different locations in the country, yet manages to avoid being touristy (no gimmicks to attract tourists, no aggressive peddling of merchandise).

Japan is beautiful no matter when you go

97% of Japan (with the exception of Okinawa, the southernmost island, and some parts of Kyushu, the second-most south island) enjoys distinct 4 seasons, and each season has a major highlight that makes choosing when to go almost too easy and too hard at once.

Spring

You have the ethereal cherry blossom trees lining the rivers and parks in full bloom (or you can wait till late spring and see lavender instead, if cherry is too "mainstream" for you).

Summer

Between July-September, you have fireworks and festivals almost every day of the week in every province, urban and rural.

Fall

From late October to the end of November, the temples and mountains are coated in amazing shades of red, orange and yellow of the autumn maple leaves.

Winter

World-class ski resorts are hustling and bustling, but if that's not for you, you can always go to the Snow Festival in Hokkaido, the northernmost island, for fun, artistic ice scultures and amazing snow crabs.

Japan is fun no matter who you are

Japan is a country rich with culture and history, so no matter what your interests are, you will find something you can have fun with. Toys from famous anime series,tranquil temples that are hundreds of years old, crazy nightlife, cutting-edge modern art, overnight hikes to see the sunrise, easy hike around the park...you get the gist.
